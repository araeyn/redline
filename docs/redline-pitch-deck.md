## Slide 2: The Problem

**Artists do not usually need more generation. They need better direction.**

- Most AI art tools focus on replacing the creative process.
- Artists often get stuck in the middle of a piece, not at the start.
- Existing feedback is slow, subjective, or hard to apply in real time.
- Many creators waste hours rendering before they know whether the piece actually reads.

**Big line on slide**

The hardest moment in art is the messy middle.  
That is where Redline helps.

**Speaker notes**

A lot of tools help you generate something from scratch, but that is not the same as helping a creator improve their own work. The real pain point is the work-in-progress stage, when an artist is asking: does this composition work, does the lighting read, where is the focal point, and what should I fix next?

---

## Slide 3: Our Solution

**Redline turns a WIP into an actionable mentor pass.**

- `Critique` surfaces composition and clarity issues directly on the artwork.
- `Relight` simulates alternate lighting directions before repainting.
- `Layers` breaks the piece into readable structural masses.
- `Framing` analyzes crop pressure and focal heat.
- `Style DNA` explains palette and visual identity.
- `Readability` stress-tests whether the idea still works at thumbnail scale.
- `Focus Region` lets artists box one problem area and get localized feedback.

**Speaker notes**

What makes Redline different is that it is not a single trick. It behaves more like a digital studio partner. Artists can get broad direction across the whole piece, then isolate one weak area and get a local mentor pass just for that region.

## Slide 5: Demo Flow

**A 45-second demo judges can follow instantly**

1. Drop in a work-in-progress sketch or painting.
2. Run `Full Mentor Pass`.
3. Show critique markers and the `Next Pass Plan`.
4. Switch to `Relight` and drag the light source.
5. Open `Readability` and show the silhouette + thumbnail ladder.
6. Use `Focus Region` on one weak area and run the local mentor pass.

**Speaker notes**

This flow matters because it shows both breadth and usefulness. It proves Redline can help with overall composition, with lighting exploration, and with targeted iteration on specific problem zones.

## Slide 6: Why Artists Would Actually Use It

**Redline saves time on the most expensive mistakes.**

- Catches weak composition before hours of rendering.
- Tests lighting without repainting from scratch.
- Shows whether the image reads at small sizes for portfolios, thumbnails, and social posts.
- Gives plain-English next steps instead of vague critique.
- Works across characters, environments, props, stylized art, and other non-anatomy-heavy pieces.

**Callout**

This is useful before posting, before painting over, and before final render.

**Speaker notes**

The goal is practical adoption. An artist should be able to use Redline during an actual workflow: check the read, test the light, mirror the piece, isolate a bad region, and decide what to fix next.

## Slide 7: Technical Depth

**Under the hood**

- Client-side visual analysis for relighting, structural layers, framing heat, and readability tests.
- Region-based critique pipeline for localized mentor passes.
- Server-side Gemini critique route so the API key stays private.

- Real-time interactions for focus boxing, mirror check, value view, and draggable lighting.

**Speaker notes**

The technical story is strong because this is not just one model call. We combine multiple analysis passes, interactive overlays, and a private server route for critique generation. It feels like a real product, not just a prompt wrapped in a UI.

## Optional Backup Slide: Judge FAQ

**Is this just for figure drawing?**

No. Redline critiques composition, value grouping, readability, framing, shape language, lighting, and style across many kinds of digital art.

**Why is this better than asking ChatGPT for feedback?**

Because the critique is visual, localized, interactive, and tied directly to the artwork through overlays, region selection, relighting, and readability analysis.

**What is the most innovative part?**

Treating AI as a real-time studio mentor rather than a generator, especially through local mentor passes and readability stress testing.

**Could artists use this outside the hackathon?**

Yes. It is most useful during the active painting and iteration process, before final rendering and before posting.
